# LifeOS Overseas Feedback Launch Playbook

> Goal: get real, high-quality feedback from English-speaking users.
> Primary audience: ADHD-ish iPhone users, low-friction journaling users, and gentle productivity communities.
> Time budget: 3-5 hours per week.

App Store URL: `https://apps.apple.com/app/lifeos/id6763877227`

---

## 1. Positioning

### Core one-liner

LifeOS is a gentle iOS app for ADHD-style brain dumps: capture messy thoughts, tasks, time blocks, moods, and reflections without streaks, shame, or tracking.

### What to emphasize

- Low-friction capture: one quick input instead of choosing the perfect category first.
- Gentle productivity: no streaks, no guilt dashboards, no "you failed" language.
- Local-first privacy: no ads, no tracking SDKs, no account required.
- Not medical: a reflection and life-tracking tool, not diagnosis, treatment, or therapy.

### Do not say

- Do not present LifeOS as a medical, therapy, treatment, or mental-health intervention.
- Do not promise ADHD symptom improvement.
- Do not say the app "understands ADHD brains" as a clinical claim; use "ADHD-style" or "ADHD-friendly" as product positioning.
- Do not enter large ADHD support communities with a product post unless the rules explicitly allow it.

## 5. Short Video Scripts

Use the same script across TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts. Keep each video 20-35 seconds. Use app screen recordings, simple captions, and a calm voiceover.

### Script 1: 12 Tabs

- Hook: `POV: your brain has 12 tabs open and no save button.`
- Voiceover: I built LifeOS for the moment when your brain throws tasks, feelings, ideas, and random reminders at you all at once. Instead of deciding where everything goes first, you brain dump. Then the app helps you organize it gently.
- Caption: `Brain dump first. Organize later.`

### Script 2: No Streaks

- Hook: `I removed streaks from my productivity app.`
- Voiceover: Streaks can motivate some people. For others, one missed day turns into shame and quitting. LifeOS is built around coming back gently, not being perfect.
- Caption: `No streaks. No shame. Just come back.`

### Script 3: Brain Dump Becomes Structure

- Hook: `This messy thought can become a task, time block, and note.`
- Voiceover: The hardest part is not doing the task. It is capturing the thought before it disappears. LifeOS lets you start messy, then turn the useful parts into structure.
- Caption: `Messy capture is still capture.`

### Script 4: No Failed Dashboard

- Hook: `What if a productivity app did not tell you that you failed?`
- Voiceover: LifeOS does not rank you, shame you, or turn missed days into a moral failure. It is a life observation system: tasks, time, moods, and reflections without the guilt layer.
- Caption: `Observation over judgment.`

### Script 5: What Apps Miss

- Hook: `What most productivity apps misunderstand about ADHD-style users.`
- Voiceover: The issue is not always motivation. Sometimes the system asks for too many decisions before capture. LifeOS lowers the first step: say the thing, save the thing, sort it later.
- Caption: `Lower the first step.`

## 6. Product Hunt Prep

**Do not launch until at least 50 people have tried the app and positioning has been validated through Reddit/X feedback.**

- Product name: `LifeOS`
- Tagline: `Brain dump first. Organize life gently.`
- Short description: `LifeOS is a gentle iOS app for ADHD-style brain dumps, tasks, daily check-ins, time blocks, moods, and reflections. No streaks, no shame, no ads, no tracking, and no account required.`

Maker comment:

```text
Hi Product Hunt,

I built LifeOS because most productivity apps ask users to be organized before they can even capture a thought.

That breaks down fast for people like me. A thought appears, I have to decide whether it is a task, note, mood, habit, journal entry, or calendar item, and then the thought is gone.

LifeOS starts from a simpler premise:

Brain dump first. Organize later.

It combines quick capture, tasks, daily check-ins, time blocks, moods, and reflection in one gentle iOS app. I intentionally avoided streaks, guilt dashboards, ads, tracking SDKs, and account creation.

This is not a medical app and it does not diagnose or treat anything. It is a daily scaffolding tool for people who want lower-friction life tracking.

I would love feedback on the onboarding, App Store positioning, and whether this feels useful beyond the first day.
```
